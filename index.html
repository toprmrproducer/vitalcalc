What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple screening measure calculated from your height and weight:weight (kg) ÷ height (m)². It's used by health organizations like theWorld Health Organization (WHO) as a quick, population-level way to flag whether someone's weight might fall into an underweight, normal, overweight, or obese range, using the standard cutoffs of18.5, 25, and 30.

Ideal weight range & BMI Prime

Your ideal weight range is the span of weights that would place your BMI in the healthy 18.5–24.9 band for your exact height. BMI Prime is your BMI divided by 25 (the upper limit of a normal BMI): a value of 1.0 sits right at that ceiling, below 1.0 is within the healthy-to-underweight range, and above 1.0 means you're over the WHO normal cutoff. It's a single ratio that shows at a glance how far you are from the top of the healthy range.

Limitations to keep in mind

BMI does not distinguish between muscle and fat, so muscular or athletic people can register as "overweight" despite having low body fat. It also doesn't account for age, sex, bone density, or where fat is distributed on the body — factors that matter for actual health risk. BMI is a useful, free, one-minute starting point, not a diagnosis. Talk to a doctor or healthcare provider for a full assessment of your health.
