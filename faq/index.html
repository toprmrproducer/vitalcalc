Frequently Asked Questions

Is VitalCalc really free?

Yes. Every calculation runs on your own device, so there is no server cost per use and no reason to charge or limit usage.

Is my height and weight uploaded anywhere?

No. Everything is calculated locally in your browser using JavaScript. Your data never leaves your device.

How is BMI calculated?

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height (m)². VitalCalc accepts either metric (cm, kg) or imperial (ft/in, lb) units and converts automatically.

What do the BMI categories mean?

VitalCalc uses the standard World Health Organization cutoffs: under 18.5 is Underweight, 18.5–24.9 is Normal weight, 25–29.9 is Overweight, and 30+ is Obese.

What is the ideal weight range?

It is the span of weights that would place your BMI in the healthy 18.5–24.9 band for your exact height, shown in whichever unit (kg or lb) you have selected.

What is BMI Prime?

BMI Prime is your BMI divided by 25, the upper limit of a normal BMI. A value of 1.0 sits right at that ceiling: below 1.0 is within the healthy-to-underweight range, and above 1.0 means you are over the WHO normal cutoff.

Is BMI a perfect measure of health?

No. BMI does not distinguish between muscle and fat, and does not account for age, sex, bone density, or fat distribution. It is a useful starting point, not a diagnosis — talk to a doctor for a full assessment.
